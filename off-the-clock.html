Off the clock

The part you don’t see on LinkedIn.

Outside of work, I’m usually training, listening to music, chasing a new obsession, or doing something slightly unproductive with friends. This page is just a more personal look at what keeps me grounded outside the work itself.

About me

The real me

Outside of work I’m a 20-year-old uni student and the big brother to three other siblings. I currently live in a flat with seven boys, which means most weeknights involve training, uni work, and watching questionable romance movies. I’m a pretty social person, so weekends are usually spent with mates and tend to be slightly unproductive. I also get pretty fixated on learning things, which means there’s normally a new hobby or interest on the go.

Overall, I’m a big believer in working hard while still making time for the things you enjoy.
Working hard matters more when you still leave room for the things that make you feel like yourself.
Usually doing
Training, editing, uni work, music, or getting overly invested in a new hobby.
Energy outside work
Social, a little chaotic, pretty curious, and normally in motion.

Sport

Kickboxing & Muay Thai

Kickboxing and Muay Thai are a huge part of my life. There is something honest about combat sport — discipline, pressure, timing, and learning to stay calm in uncomfortable moments.

Training and fighting keeps my mind sharp and grounded outside creative work. It gives me structure, clears my head, and reminds me that progress usually comes from repetition more than anything dramatic.
