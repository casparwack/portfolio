The real me

I’m at my best when I’m building something: a story, a brand, a training block, a camera setup, a plan that turns chaos into momentum. I like work that feels cinematic — not “flashy”, but intentional. The kind of creative that has silence in it.

I’m obsessive (in a good way) about detail: pacing, tone, what’s left unsaid. I’ll happily spend an hour moving one line of copy if it changes how the whole thing feels. That same energy shows up outside work too — music on repeat, film scenes I remember years later, and combat sport as a reset button.

Kickboxing & Muay Thai

Combat sport is my favourite kind of honest. No algorithm. No shortcuts. Just reps, timing, pressure, and learning how to stay calm when it matters. It’s the best balance to marketing: one is all perception, the other is brutally real.

I love the discipline, the theatre, and the humility of it — you earn every improvement. And weirdly, it makes my creative work better: sharper decisions, less overthinking, more follow-through.
